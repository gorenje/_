The Presidents Prints

Tour

Fingerprints pen-plots are presented with the object upon which they were found. Objects were collected in public spaces and represent everyday items used by each of us, potentially daily.

Each pen-plot is a 30 cm by 40 cm fingerprint vectorisation on 300 gram paper. Each plot is assigned an eight digit hexadecimal number which becomes its name.

The representation of a fingerprint as a hexadecimal number is a reminder that digital devices do not store images of fingerprints, instead devices store a cryptological representation called hash.

A specific fingerprint can generate a specific hash but a specific hash cannot recreate the fingerprint that generated it.

Thus stolen devices do not allow the reconstruction of the fingerprint that allows the device to be used. There is no backward relationship between a hash and the fingerprint that generated it.

The title The Presidents Prints is a reminder that even the most important people have fingerprints, fingerprints that they also use. Convenience is a universal human desire.
