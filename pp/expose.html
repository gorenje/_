The Presidents Prints

Exposé

Finger prints are ubiquitous, prints left on everything we touch - glasses, bottles, plates, phones, cutlery ... We leave them in plain sight and trust those that find them not to abuse our trust.

We utilise our finger prints do banking, to find the perfect partner or purchase our food online. Here we forget the universality of finger prints.

Our finger prints have become our unalterable digital passwords. Passwords that we do not need to remember, passwords that seemingly do not need to be altered.

Combining analogy, digital and artificial technologies, The Presidents Prints highlights our reliance on a form of insecure digital security.
